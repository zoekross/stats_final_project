Describing Data and Research Question:

Our data is an analysis of Bob Rosss paintings that show when certain elements of nature such as trees or waves appear in his work. This dataset comes from the fivethirtyeight package in R studio and it was collected by counting the number of certain objects in 403 of his paintings. We hope to learn more about how specific elements in his paintings influence the likelihood that a tree will appear in that same artwork.

Question: We will work to examine how the frequencies of specific variables in his paintings affect the likelihood of other elements appearing in his works.

On average, the presence of a lake in a painting increases the odds of having a tree in the painting by 26.584474. This makes sense and is in accordance with what we expected from the graph above. There is only one lake in all of his paintings, which doesnt have a tree paired with it. We chose to fit this model because our graph of tree and lake had an interesting relationship that we wanted to investigate further.

glm_cloud <- glm(tree ~ lake+clouds,

We chose to produce this model because we wanted to see if adding an extra independent variable is going to change the relationship between lake and tree. By adding the additional independent variable, the odds for the lake coefficient increase to 28.21, on average. The presence of clouds in a painting increases the odds of a tree being present in the painting by 0.20, on average.
